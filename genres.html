Classical Music

Classical music is art music produced or rooted in the traditions of Western culture, including both liturgical and secular music. While a more precise term is also used to refer to the period from 1750 to 1820 (the Classical period), this article is about the broad span of time from before the 6th century AD to the present day.

Classical music is characterized by complexity and sophistication, with long-standing traditions of notation, instrumental and vocal pieces, symphonies, operas, and solo performances.

Key Composers

  • Johann Sebastian Bach (1685-1750)
  • Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart (1756-1791)
  • Ludwig van Beethoven (1770-1827)
  • Frédéric Chopin (1810-1849)
  • Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky (1840-1893)

Jazz

Jazz is a music genre that originated in the African-American communities of New Orleans, Louisiana, in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, with its roots in blues and ragtime. Since the 1920s Jazz Age, it has been recognized as a major form of musical expression in traditional and popular music.

Jazz is characterized by swing and blue notes, complex chords, call and response vocals, polyrhythms, and improvisation. It has been described as "America's classical music."

Key Musicians

  • Louis Armstrong (1901-1971)
  • Duke Ellington (1899-1974)
  • Miles Davis (1926-1991)
  • John Coltrane (1926-1967)
  • Ella Fitzgerald (1917-1996)

Rock Music

Rock music is a broad genre of popular music that originated as "rock and roll" in the United States in the late 1940s and early 1950s, developing into a range of different styles in the mid-1960s and later, particularly in the United States and the United Kingdom.

Rock music is traditionally centered on the electric guitar, usually as part of a rock group with electric bass, drums, and one or more singers. The sound of rock often revolves around the electric guitar or acoustic guitar, and it uses a strong back beat laid down by a rhythm section.

Key Bands and Artists

  • The Beatles (1960-1970)
  • Led Zeppelin (1968-1980)
  • Queen (1970-present)
  • Pink Floyd (1965-1995, 2005, 2012-2014)
  • The Rolling Stones (1962-present)

Electronic Music

Electronic music is music that employs electronic musical instruments, digital instruments, or circuitry-based music technology in its creation. It includes both music made using electronic and electromechanical means.

Electronic music began in the late 19th century and by the 1950s, electronic instruments and electronic music technology were being progressively introduced to wider audiences. Today, electronic music includes many varieties and ranges from experimental art music to popular forms such as electronic dance music (EDM).

Key Artists

  • Kraftwerk (1970-present)
  • Jean-Michel Jarre (1968-present)
  • Daft Punk (1993-2021)
  • Aphex Twin (1985-present)
  • Brian Eno (1971-present)

World Music

World music is a musical category encompassing many different styles of music from around the world, including traditional music, quasi-traditional music, and music where more than one cultural tradition intermingle.

The term was popularized in the 1980s as a marketing category for non-Western traditional music. It has grown to include subgenres such as ethnic fusion and worldbeat.

Key Musical Traditions

  • Indian Classical (Hindustani and Carnatic)
  • African Drumming and Vocal Traditions
  • Celtic Folk Music
  • Middle Eastern and Arabic Music
  • Traditional Japanese Music
